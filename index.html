A place for enthusiasts of J.R.R. Tolkien's invented languages

Mae govannen and mára omentië! Among these pages you'll find many articles relating to the Elven tongues of Arda, the invented world of J.R.R. Tolkien. Sindarin (or Grey-elven), my favorite of the languages, is especially treated.

Essays on The Languages of J.R.R. Tolkien

  • Elvish Is Dead; Long Live Elvish - A memoir of my experiences with Tolkien's languages, reflecting on why you can't (and can) and should (and shouldn't) learn Elvish. Read this first if you want to understand what you're getting yourself into.
  • Concerning Stress Placement in Sindarin - I explore the implications for Sindarin pronunciation of two poems from The Lord of the Rings.

A Ranger's Guide to Sindarin

  • A Ranger's Guide is a description of Sindarin grammar and vocabulary, focusing on the examples (mainly in LotR) that J.R.R.T. chose to publish. I can't call it a course in the language, because you won't come out of it speaking Sindarin, at least not in the way you speak English now. It's more of a map of the territory, a guide to being at home in the Grey-elven tongue.

External Links of Interest

  • Elvish as She Is Spoke, by Carl F. Hostetter - An illuminating exposé of the difficulties facing the aspiring student of Elvish composition, and a cogent suggestion for how to face them. Hosted by the Elvish Linguistic Fellowship.
  • Ardalambion (Of the Tongues of Arda) - Helge Fauskanger's site covering many aspects of Tolkien's linguistic creations. His Quenya language course intended for beginners can be found there.
  • Parma Tyelpelassiva (Book of Silver Leaves) - Thorsten Renk's site containing Sindarin and Quenya courses, and many fan-made compositions in Elvish.
